Weston Hirschauer

Will put picture of weston here

Weston Hirschauer is the founder, owner, and head coach of Good Lift Performance, an online powerlifting coaching company. During his sophomore year of college at Iowa State University, Weston joined the weightlifting club and was introduced to powerlifting. In the fall of 2015, he competed in his first powerlifting competition and has been passionate about the sport ever since. He started coaching his friends from the club for free in 2016 to gain hands-on experience. News of his athletes success started to spread and his roster continued to grow. Weston graduated from Iowa State University in 2018 with a Bachelors of Science in Exercise Science with six different internships during his four years of undergrad ranging from collegiate strength and conditioning to personal training and corporate wellness. In 2020 Weston decided to leave the corporate fitness world and go full time into coaching for powerlifting. Weston's most notable strengths as a coach are being able to think outside the box and attention to detail. Being a low responder to training, he has had to learn to optimize every aspect of his own training to make even the smallest jumps in progress. This niche in his own training ended up being a huge advantage to his athletes. Learning everything he could about programming, technique coaching, and leadership has enabled him to gain a deep pool of knowledge that allows him to get the most out of his athletes. Weston specializes in getting stubborn lifts to progress when they don't respond to conventional training methods.
